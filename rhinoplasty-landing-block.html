Types of Rhinoplasty

  • Primary Rhinoplasty: Your first nose job, addressing cosmetic concerns, functional breathing issues, or both. Dr. Dhir tailors each primary rhinoplasty to enhance your natural features while maintaining facial balance.
  • Revision Rhinoplasty: Correcting previous nose surgery results. Revision rhinoplasty requires advanced expertise to address scar tissue, structural changes, and aesthetic refinements.
  • Ethnic Rhinoplasty: Preserving cultural characteristics while refining nasal features. Ethnic rhinoplasty respects your heritage while achieving your aesthetic goals.
  • Septoplasty / Functional: Improving breathing and airflow. Often combined with cosmetic rhinoplasty for comprehensive nasal improvement.
  • Non-Surgical (filler): Temporary nasal contouring using dermal fillers for minor adjustments without surgery.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the recovery like after rhinoplasty?

Recovery varies by individual and procedure type. Most patients experience swelling and bruising that peak within the first few days and gradually subside. A nasal splint is typically worn for about a week. Most patients can return to light activities within a week and resume normal routines within 2-3 weeks, though final results may take several months to fully settle.

How much downtime does rhinoplasty require?

Most patients need about 7-10 days of downtime before returning to work or social activities. Strenuous exercise should be avoided for 3-4 weeks. Dr. Dhir will provide specific post-operative instructions tailored to your procedure and recovery progress.

Will there be scars? (closed vs open rhinoplasty)

In closed rhinoplasty, all incisions are made inside the nostrils, leaving no visible external scars. Open rhinoplasty involves a small incision on the columella (the strip of skin between the nostrils), which heals as a nearly imperceptible line. Dr. Dhir will recommend the technique that best serves your goals—closed for simpler cases, open when more visibility is needed for complex tip work or revision rhinoplasty.

When can I exercise after rhinoplasty?

Light activities like walking can resume within a few days. Avoid strenuous exercise, heavy lifting, and activities that could impact your nose for 3-4 weeks. Dr. Dhir will assess your healing during follow-up visits and provide clearance for gradually returning to your full exercise routine.

How is rhinoplasty cost determined?

Rhinoplasty cost varies based on procedure complexity, whether it's primary or revision rhinoplasty, if septoplasty is included, anesthesia fees, and facility costs. During your consultation, Dr. Dhir will provide a detailed, transparent quote for your personalized treatment plan. We offer financing options to make your procedure more accessible.
